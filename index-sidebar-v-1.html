Sidebar, Vertical then Horizontal

This serie shows a sidebar layout. A sidebar layout consists of two elements, a sidebar and a non-sidebar. A specific constraint applies between these two elements: the orientation of the two elements changes, depending on a measurement of the sidebar in relation to that of the non-sidebar. If that relation changes, then the orientation changes.

In this serie, the sidebar layout is put into a flex container of tw-flex-v, where the orientation is column wise and the dependent measurement is the width.

If the measurement of the sidebarReset is not taller than the measurement of the non-sidebarReset , those two elements have a vertical orientation.

But if the sidebarReset is taller than the non-sidebarReset , those two elements have a horizontal orientation.

In both examples, a and b, the container of the sidebar and non-sidebar has a fixed measurement of height: 14rem; width: 35rem and a margin based on the Tailwind CSS spacing scale No. 2 (2 * 0,25rem = 0,5rem). The calculation formula of the flex-basis, by which the relation of the measurement and hence the orientation changes, is shown as text of Example a, respectively inside of each sidebar and non-sidebar.

An additional finding must be explained - as also observed by blog post Flexbox gutters and negative margins. The application of a negative margins around a flex item like a-1-sidebar-conReset , being part of a flex container of vertical orientation like a-1Reset , overflows the flex container! As a workaround, a wrapper like fixes_mar_in_flex_vReset applying tw-flex-h tw-m-2 helps, as a flex box of orientation row does not suffer from this margin behaviour. Additionally as counter measure, a positive margin on fixes_mar_in_flex_vReset must be assigned by the same magnitude as is applied on the a-1-sidebar-conReset .
