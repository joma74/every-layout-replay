Every Layout - The Center, Vertical And Horizontal, By Flex, By Container

This serie shows how to horizontal and vertical center elements by applying alignments to a flex box and either distribute them evenly or uniform.

1-h-center-vhReset shows an application of tw-flex-h tw-center-vh-byflex-myon the container of a button, which is oriented in a direction of column.

2-v-center-vhReset shows an application of tw-flex-v tw-center-vh-byflex-myon the container of a button, which is oriented in a direction of row.

3-h-center-vh-uniformReset shows an application of tw-flex-h tw-center-vh-byflex-myon the container of two buttons, which is oriented in a direction of row. To distribute the buttons uniform along the v line, a class of tw-v-dist-uniform-my is applied on the container.

4-h-center-vh-evenlyReset shows an application of tw-flex-h tw-center-h-byflex-myon the container of two buttons, which is oriented in a direction of row. To distribute the buttons evenly along the v line, a class of tw-dist-v-evenly-my is applied on the container.

5-v-center-vh-uniformReset shows an application of tw-flex-v tw-center-vh-byflex-myon the container of two buttons, which is oriented in a direction of column. To distribute the buttons uniform along the v line, a class of tw-v-dist-uniform-my is applied on the container.

6-v-center-h-evenlyReset shows an application of tw-flex-v tw-center-v-byflex-myon the container of two buttons, which is oriented in a direction of column. To distribute the buttons evenly along the v line, a class of tw-dist-v-evenly-my is applied on the container.

This element will be replaced by any element iff a root element of id usage-example exists and having an attribute of for-code-example. Then any node having an attribute of for-code-example is selected, formatted and highlighted here.

A class of tw-flex-1 on each example is applied only to make those, being each a flex-item themselves, the same height. If there is either negative or postive space to be distributed, then regardless of their individual natural size, after distributing the space proportional to the specified flex factor, the one with the maximal min-size dictates the effective size of the others.
