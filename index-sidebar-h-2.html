Sidebar, Horizontal then Vertical, Spec Bug

This serie shows a sidebar layout. A sidebar layout consists of two elements, a sidebar and a non-sidebar. A specific constraint applies between these two elements: the orientation of the two elements changes, depending on a measurement of the sidebar in relation to that of the non-sidebar. If that relation changes, then the orientation changes.

In this serie, the sidebar layout is put into a flex container of tw-flex-h plus being made wrap-able, where the dependent measurement is the height.

In both examples, a and b, the container of the sidebar and non-sidebar has a fixed measurement of height: 14rem; width: 35rem and a margin based on the Tailwind CSS spacing scale No. 2 (2 * 0,25rem = 0,5rem). The calculation formula of the flex-basis, by which the relation of the measurement and hence the orientation changes, is shown as text of Example a, respectively inside of each sidebar and non-sidebar.

Now turning to the spec bug. The spec bug can be observed in a-2-sidebar-nonReset . The element overflows it's flex container a-2-sidebar-conReset , as soon as the sidebar is made taller in height than the sidebar-non, hence wrapping occurs. As summarized in When flexbox items wrap in column mode, container does not grow its width

This looks like a fundamental deficiency in flex layout. A flex container in column-direction will not expand to accommodate additional columns.
