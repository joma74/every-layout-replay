Flexbox, Fill Layout

This serie shows how a column directed flex box is converted to a Fill Layout.

The first step, step-1Reset , shows the initial state, which is a flex-box of direction column. Note that such a direction of column displays it's items row-wise. Note also, that the contained elements step-1-elm-1Reset and step-1-elm-2Reset are no flex-items, as step-1Reset itself is not a flex-box.

step-2Reset then transforms the container step-2Reset into a flex-box of direction row. The contained elements step-2-elm-1Reset and step-2-elm-2Reset are now flex-items, which, by default, stretch against their cross-axis, which is of vertical orientation.

step-3Reset then transforms step-3-elm-1Reset and step-3-elm-2Reset themselves into flex-box of direction row. They still are of horizontal orientation, but as the initial value of a flex-box of 0 1 auto does not allow their flex items (in this case text nodes) to grow, those only take their natural space. They still, by default, stretch against their cross-axis, which is of vertical orientation.

step-4Reset then applies tw-flex-grow on each step-4-elm-1Reset and step-4-elm-2Reset allowing them to grow their items (in this case text nodes) into the space available in their horizontal orientation.

This element will be replaced by any element iff a root element of id usage-example exists and having an attribute of for-code-example. Then any node having an attribute of for-code-example is selected, formatted and highlighted here.

A class of tw-flex-1 on each step-* container is applied only to make those, being each a flex-item themselves, the same height. If there is either negative or postive space to be distributed, then regardless of their individual natural size, after distributing the space proportional to the specified flex factor, the one with the maximal min-size dictates the effective size of the others.
